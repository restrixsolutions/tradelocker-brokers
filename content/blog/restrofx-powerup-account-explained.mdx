## How the "Trade Like You Have More" Math Actually Works

Two things are happening when you trade on a PowerUp account:

1. **The bonus increases your equity** (and therefore your free margin), letting you open more or larger positions before hitting margin call.
2. **Broker leverage** then multiplies your buying power into notional exposure.

A worked example for a $1,000 deposit on EUR/USD:

- Cash deposit: $1,000
- Bonus credit: $1,250
- Total equity: $2,250
- Leverage on forex majors: **1:100**
- Maximum notional position size: $2,250 × 100 = **$225,000**

That's where RestroFX's "Trade like you have $225,000" marketing line comes from — it's notional exposure via leverage, not literal account value. It's a real number, but it's leverage doing the heavy lifting, not the bonus alone. **The bonus is what funds the margin to support that exposure**; without it, the same $1,000 deposit would only support $100,000 notional at 1:100.

## The Five Things You Need to Know Before Depositing

This is the section that most affiliate-driven write-ups skip. If you only read one part of this guide, read this.

### 1. The bonus is **never** withdrawable — not now, not ever

The 125% credit only exists to inflate your margin. It does not pay out. It does not convert to cash. Profits you make trading **with** the bonus are yours to withdraw, but the bonus credit itself never leaves the account in cash form.

### 2. Withdrawing **cash** removes the bonus until you re-deposit

If you deposit $1,000, get $1,250 bonus, grow your account to $3,000, and then withdraw $500 — RestroFX will remove the bonus credit from your account. You still keep your $500 withdrawal and your trading profits, but your remaining balance loses the bonus inflation. Re-depositing brings the bonus back.

This means PowerUp **rewards consistent compounding** and **penalises frequent withdrawals**. If your trading rhythm involves pulling profits every two weeks, PowerUp probably isn't your account.

### 3. Tighter leverage caps will surprise high-leverage traders

1:100 on majors is half of what RAW gives you, and 1:3 on BTC versus 1:10 is a real constraint for crypto traders. Check your normal position sizing before assuming PowerUp is just "free money."

### 4. Commission is $6 RT — not free

PowerUp is a **fee-bearing** account. Every lot you trade pays $6 round turn on top of the spread markup. On heavy volume that adds up fast. It is comparable to a standard ECN account but **not** comparable to a zero-commission Standard account.

### 5. There is no turnover requirement, but there is no free lunch either

You don't have to trade X lots to "unlock" the bonus — it works as buying power from the moment it's credited. But you are paying for that bonus indirectly through wider markups, higher commission, tighter leverage, and earlier stop-outs. RestroFX is not giving anything away; they are repackaging the cost into the trading conditions.
