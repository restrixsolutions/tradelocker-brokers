## What is ECN Execution?

### ECN vs Market Maker Models

**ECN (Electronic Communication Network):**
- Your orders route directly to liquidity providers (banks, hedge funds, other traders)
- Broker earns commission, not spread markup
- True market spreads (often 0.0 pips)
- No conflict of interest (broker doesn't profit from your losses)
- Direct market access (DMA)

**Market Maker (Dealing Desk):**
- Broker takes the opposite side of your trades
- Broker profits when you lose
- Wider spreads (broker's markup)
- Potential conflict of interest
- Orders may not reach real market

### Why ECN Matters for Scalping

**Speed:** ECN brokers offer faster execution (30-50ms) vs market makers (100-200ms). When scalping 20-30 trades per day, milliseconds matter.

**Transparency:** Real market spreads mean no hidden costs. You see exactly what you're paying.

**No Requotes:** ECN brokers rarely requote because orders go to actual market. Market makers requote frequently during fast markets.

**Better Fills:** ECN execution provides price improvement opportunities. Sometimes you get filled at better prices than requested.

**Scalping-Friendly:** Many market makers restrict or ban scalping. ECN brokers welcome scalpers (they earn commission regardless).
