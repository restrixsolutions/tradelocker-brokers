## GatesFX Regulation — The Full Truth

This is where most reviews go soft. We will not.

GatesFX advertises two things on the regulation front: an **FSCA registration in South Africa** and a **St. Lucia IBC**. Both are real. Neither is what most traders assume they are.

### 1. The St. Lucia IBC is not a forex license

GatesFX is incorporated as an International Business Company (IBC) in St. Lucia. An IBC is a corporate structure — it gives the company legal existence and tax treatment, the same way a Delaware LLC does in the US.

**It is not a forex broker license.** The St. Lucia International Financial Centre (IFC) has stated publicly, in its own FAQ, that it does **not license or regulate forex brokers**. So any marketing that frames the St. Lucia presence as "regulation" or "licensed in St. Lucia" is at best misleading and at worst deceptive.

What this means practically: if a serious dispute arises, you have no St. Lucia regulator to escalate to. There is no forex-specific supervisor in that jurisdiction.

### 2. The FSCA registration is real — but limited in scope

GatesFX is registered with South Africa's **Financial Sector Conduct Authority (FSCA) under FSP No. 46087**. This is verifiable on the FSCA's public register and is the real regulatory anchor for the firm.

FSCA is a legitimate regulator. It enforces conduct standards, requires fund segregation, and can sanction firms. However, two things matter for non-South-African traders:

- The FSCA's scope is **conduct of business and consumer protection within South Africa**. Its enforcement reach over offshore trading services delivered to clients in other countries is materially weaker than tier-1 regimes like FCA (UK), ASIC (Australia), or CySEC (EU).
- FSCA is **not** considered a tier-1 forex regulator by most independent ranking bodies. It is a tier-2/tier-3 framework — better than no regulation, weaker than the top tier.

### 3. What is not there: tier-1 oversight

GatesFX does **not** hold:

- FCA (UK) authorization
- ASIC (Australia) license
- CySEC (EU) license
- NFA / CFTC registration (US)

That is the honest picture. GatesFX is a real, registered broker with a legitimate FSCA footprint. It is not a top-tier-regulated broker, and any marketing language that suggests "fully regulated" or "licensed in St. Lucia" should be read carefully.

**Bottom line:** GatesFX is more transparent than a fully offshore "license" broker, less protective than an FCA/ASIC broker. Size your account accordingly.
