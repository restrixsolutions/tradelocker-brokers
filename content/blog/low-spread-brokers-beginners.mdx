## What Are Spreads and Why Do They Matter?

### Understanding Spreads (Simple Explanation)

The **spread** is the difference between the buy price (ask) and sell price (bid) of a currency pair. It's how brokers make money on each trade you place.

**Example:**
- EUR/USD **Buy Price (Ask):** 1.10012
- EUR/USD **Sell Price (Bid):** 1.10000
- **Spread:** 1.2 pips

**What This Means:**
The moment you buy EUR/USD at 1.10012, you're immediately down 1.2 pips because you can only sell at 1.10000. The price must move 1.2 pips in your favor just to break even.

### Why Spreads Matter More for Beginners

**1. Smaller Profits = Spreads Take Bigger Percentage**

**Example Trade:**
- Target: 10 pips profit
- Spread: 1.5 pips
- Spread cost: 15% of your profit

**High Spread Broker (3.0 pips):**
- Target: 10 pips
- Spread: 3.0 pips
- Spread cost: 30% of your profit
- Must move 13 pips to make 10 pips profit

**2. Lower Win Rates**
Beginners typically have 40-50% win rates while learning. High spreads make it nearly impossible to be profitable with these win rates.

**3. More Trades = More Costs**
Beginners often overtrade while learning. Each trade costs you the spread, which adds up quickly.

**Monthly Cost Example (30 trades):**
- **Low spread (1.0 pip):** 30 pips Ã— $10 = $300
- **High spread (3.0 pips):** 90 pips Ã— $10 = $900
- **Difference:** $600/month wasted

### Types of Spreads

**Fixed Spreads:**
- Same spread regardless of market conditions
- Typically wider (1.5-3.0 pips on EUR/USD)
- Predictable costs
- Better for beginners (no surprises)

**Variable Spreads:**
- Change based on liquidity and volatility
- Can be very tight (0.0-0.5 pips during liquid hours)
- Can widen significantly (2.0-5.0 pips during news/low liquidity)
- Better for experienced traders who trade during optimal hours

**ECN/Raw Spreads:**
- True market spreads (often 0.0 pips)
- Broker charges separate commission ($5-$7 per lot)
- Total cost often lower than fixed spreads
- Better for active traders

## Beginner Trading Mistakes to Avoid

### Mistake #1: Overleveraging

**The Trap:**
Broker offers 1:500 leverage. Beginner thinks "I can control $50,000 with $100!" Then trades 1 standard lot on $100 account. One 10-pip move = account blown.

**Reality:**
Leverage amplifies losses as much as profits. High leverage with poor risk management = quick account wipeout.

**Solution:**
- Use only 1:50 to 1:100 leverage (even if higher available)
- Risk only 1-2% per trade
- Start with micro lots (0.01)
- Calculate position size before every trade

**Example (Proper Risk Management):**
- $500 account
- Risk 2% = $10 per trade
- 20-pip stop loss
- Position size: $10 Ã· 20 pips Ã· $1/pip = 0.1 lots

### Mistake #2: Trading Without a Plan

**The Trap:**
Opening charts, "feeling" like EUR/USD will go up, placing random trade without analysis.

**Reality:**
Emotional, unplanned trading has near 0% success rate. You're gambling, not trading.

**Solution:**
Create a simple trading plan:
1. What pairs will you trade? (Start with EUR/USD only)
2. What timeframes? (Start with 4H or daily)
3. What's your strategy? (Trend following, support/resistance, etc.)
4. What's your risk per trade? (1-2%)
5. When will you exit? (Preset stop loss and take profit)

Write it down. Follow it every trade.

### Mistake #3: Ignoring Risk Management

**The Trap:**
Trading without stop losses, risking 20% per trade, "I'll just watch it and close if it goes wrong."

**Reality:**
Losses will happen. Without risk management, one bad trade (or streak) wipes out your account.

**Solution:**
- **Always use stop losses** (no exceptions)
- **Never risk more than 2% per trade** (1% better for beginners)
- **Use proper position sizing** (calculate before every trade)
- **Accept losses** (they're part of trading)

**Risk Management Formula:**
```
Position Size = (Account Size Ã— Risk %) Ã· (Stop Loss in Pips Ã— Pip Value)
```

### Mistake #4: Overtrading

**The Trap:**
Taking 20-30 trades per day trying to make money fast. Each trade costs spreads, adding up to hundreds of dollars monthly.

**Reality:**
More trades â‰  more profit. Quality > quantity. Overtrading guarantees losses from spreads alone.

**Solution:**
- Limit yourself to 2-5 trades per day maximum
- Wait for high-probability setups
- Be patient (missing a trade is better than forcing bad trades)
- Track your stats (you'll see fewer, better trades = more profit)

### Mistake #5: Chasing Losses

**The Trap:**
Losing 3 trades in a row, getting frustrated, doubling position size to "make it back quickly." Result: bigger losses.

**Reality:**
Losing streaks happen to everyone (even profitable traders). Emotional trading makes it worse.

**Solution:**
- Accept that losses are normal
- Stick to your risk management (don't increase size after losses)
- Take a break after 3 losing trades (walk away, clear your head)
- Review your trades (learn from mistakes instead of repeating them)
